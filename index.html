Part 01

The Root of Confusion About Purpose

Why do most of us struggle with the question: "Why was I born? What am I living for?"

🏠 Generational Influence

Most of us identified parents as our strongest life influence. But the previous generation operated with limited knowledge and no concept of lifelong learning. They studied till college, then worked 30-40 years with only news as updates. Their advice, while well-intentioned, was based on limited exposure and boiled down to one message: "If you study, you will get good work."

💬 The Fear Conditioning

We were raised on fear: "No job, no respect." "They'll call you a fool." This fear was repeated so often it became ingrained. The focus was always on securing a job, never on discovering what you actually want to do or who you want to become.

We don't know what we want, there is no one to teach us, our home situation is not like that, and all the confused people in our life are completely stuck. When everyone around is confused, how can they give you clarity?
Part 02

Information Overload & Shiny Object Syndrome

When there was only one option, there was no confusion. Now there are countless possibilities, and we're drowning in them.

🌐 Too Much, Too Fast

With the internet and social media, knowledge that was previously available to only a few became widely accessible. This abundance led to Shiny Object Syndrome: everything looks good, everything seems like an opportunity. We get bored easily, jump from one thing to another, and end up going nowhere.

🔄 The Template That Sticks

In college, we developed a pattern: try something, put in effort, reduce it to zero, move to something new. This became a template we carry into adult life. Starting things, not finishing them, and constantly searching for the next opportunity. This template repeats in hobbies, jobs, businesses, and relationships.

The gap between expectation and reality becomes the pressure of life. We need to consciously debug these patterns.
Part 03

Survival Mode vs. Purpose

When you're in survival mode, you can't see the big picture. And without the big picture, there's no alignment or purpose.

⚠ Fear-Induced Living

Post-college, many of us enter survival mode: a fear-induced state where the focus is purely on managing and surviving rather than thriving. We learn to safeguard ourselves, going one step back instead of forward. This mode is meant to be temporary, but many stay trapped in it for years.

When you are in survival mode, you don't see big picture. The alignment will change according to the person who sees big picture. Only those who can see the big picture can find alignment and develop purpose in life.

🔒 The Three Stress Responses

Like an animal facing a predator, we go through: Freeze (realize what happened), Flight (try to run), or Fight (attack back). When life keeps us in this mechanism permanently, constant stress becomes our default state, preventing any clear thinking about purpose.

Part 04

Maslow's Hierarchy & Basic Needs

Before thinking about purpose, basic necessities must be met. Most people are stuck in the first two or three layers.

Self-Actualization Achieving full potential, creativity Self-fulfillment
Esteem Needs Prestige, accomplishment, recognition Psychological needs
Belongingness & Love Relationships, friends, community
Safety Needs Security, stability, employment Basic needs
Physiological Needs Food, water, home, medical, clothing
I am having a hard time for survival. I am having self doubt questions like, 'Will I be able to work today?' I can't do anything if I want to go there. So, somewhere I am stuck in this.

💡 Key Insight

Before finding purpose, you must find what you like so you can create an environment where you can think without stress. Once you find a field you're serious about, the environment becomes ready and eventually purpose will be defined. Don't skip steps.

Part 05

Purpose Evolves. It Doesn't Find You.

You cannot set one big purpose for your entire life. Purpose is not a destination. It's a direction that shifts as you grow.

College Years

Purpose was simply to get a job. Survive the interview process. Get placed.

Early Career

Purpose shifted to finding a job I actually liked, not just any job.

Exploration Phase

Started exploring education, business, and marketing. 3 years of experimenting.

Community Building

Started 21DC (21 Days Challenge), which evolved into digital initiatives, events, and the Tamil Nadu Digital Summit.

Today

Looking back, everything is interconnected. Each stage prepared the next. But it was never visible in the moment.

🎯 Start with Temporary Purpose

If you think something is your purpose, consider it temporarily. Whether it's right or wrong doesn't matter at this stage. It's a good start. Eventually, it will evolve. Don't wait for the "perfect" purpose to appear. Act on what feels right now.

Part 06

The Passion Trap

Following your passion blindly is dangerous advice. Passion needs direction and usefulness to become purpose.

❌

Useless Passion

You love watching Reels all day. You're passionate about it. But the usefulness is close to zero. Following this passion is not related to purpose.

✅

Useful Passion

You start creating valuable Reels content, build a community, generate impact. Now the passion has moved from useless to useful.

Two Requirements for Passion to Become Purpose

1. You must genuinely love and appreciate the activity enough to sustain it long-term.

2. The passion must serve a useful purpose, create value, or solve a real problem for others.

Part 07

The Three Es

Life is about three key activities that work together to create real education. Not classroom education, but life education.

🧪

Experimenting

Testing ideas and trying new things. Building websites, starting projects, testing hypotheses. Action over analysis.

🌍

Exploring

Getting out of your comfort zone. Traveling, meeting people, observing businesses. Expanding your point of view.

📚

Educating

Learning through books, documentaries, long-form content. Continuous, self-directed learning beyond formal education.

🧭 Use Hypothesis in Decision-Making

Assume your decision is right and take action based on that assumption. "I am going to experiment with this for the next two years." But don't be reckless. Experiment without disturbing your base. Keep your backup plan while exploring. In India, if you figure out how to do business while maintaining responsibilities, you gain a unique strength that translates globally.

If you do business in India, you will do business in the world. If we figure out this from India, then we have a great power.
Part 08

A Framework for Finding Purpose

Three questions that, when they intersect, reveal your opportunity and purpose.

01

What are you good at?

Your skills, knowledge, and expertise. What comes naturally to you? What have you developed over years?

02

What problems are you solving?

The pain points you can address. Real problems in the real market that people actually face.

03

Who are you helping?

Your target audience. Specific people whose lives improve because of your work.

⚡ Live Example from the Workshop

Skill: AI Knowledge
→
Problem: Kids can't learn AI in school
→
Solution: Train children in AI skills

Purpose Found
When you have a skill and see a real market problem that you can solve, this creates your opportunity and purpose.
Part 09

Energy Gain vs. Energy Loss

The ultimate test of whether you're living your purpose: does the work give you energy or drain it?

⚡

Living Your Purpose

You get energy from the work. Time flies. You can sustain it for years. 6 years of community building, experiments, courses, and cohorts, all powered by passion.

🔋

Just Surviving

Every task drains you. You watch the clock. You need external motivation to keep going. Burnout becomes inevitable.

By living your purpose, you will get energy. If you are passionate about certain things, when you do that work, you won't have energy loss. You will have energy gain.
Important Warning

Context Matters

⚠ Don't Copy Without Context

People watch motivational videos and feel they should act with courage, without considering context. Someone who succeeded 30 years ago operated in a different field, different market, and different circumstances. We need to understand: their situation, business context, backup support, and how many times they failed. Inspiration and motivation are useful, but without understanding context, people get lost.

Q&A Highlights

Real Questions, Real Answers

💬 "I know web development but want to start a business. Should I leave my job?"
Don't quit. Experiment without disturbing your current work. Focus on Shopify website development, since D2C brands need them and pay 1.5-2 lakhs per project. Use your 4 Saturdays (effectively 6 days per month). Handle 2 projects per month. If you can earn solid income for 3-4 months, then consider scaling. The formula: experiment on weekends, prove the model, then decide.
💬 "I'm 56, own a construction company. My family says I should rest after 35 years. What's my purpose now?"
The response was simple and powerful: "Are you feeling painful or happy? I am enjoying. That's all." Your life itself is an enjoyment. There's no fixed meaning to "taking rest." If you enjoy what you're doing, keep doing it. Don't let others define when you should stop.

🎯 Your Action Items

Write down 3 things you're good at and identify 5 problems in your daily life to find potential opportunities where your skills meet real needs.

Experiment on weekends without disturbing your current stability. Aim for small wins: 2 projects per month in your area of interest to build evidence that it works.

Start exploring through the Three Es: Experiment with new ideas, Explore by getting out of your comfort zone, and Educate yourself through books and long-form content.
